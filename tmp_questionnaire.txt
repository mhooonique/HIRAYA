Q3.  What specific clauses or disclosures should be added to the Terms of Service to ensure 
the platform's compliance with applicable Philippine laws and DOST institutional policies?  
Answer / Feedback: 
 
1. Data Privacy and User Information 
Clause 1.1: Scope of Data Collection. The Digital Platform collects and processes only the 
personal information required for account creation and identity verification. By registering, 
the User consents to the storage of this login information for the purpose of secure system 
access and communication. 
Clause 1.2: Data Retention and Deletion. User login information shall be retained only for 
the duration of the account's active status. Upon a written request for account termination, all 
personal identifiers shall be purged from the system database within thirty (30) days, in 
compliance with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) standards. 
2. Intellectual Property and Technology Commercialization 
Clause 2.1: Innovator’s Warranty of Ownership. By uploading innovative technologies for 
adoption, the Innovator warrants that they hold the legal rights or authorized license to the 
intellectual property. The Innovator assumes all liability for the accuracy of technical 
specifications and for ensuring that the commercialization of such technology does not 
infringe on existing patents. 
Clause 2.2: Public vs. Restricted Visibility. Basic technology descriptions and high-level 
benefits are treated as "Public Information" to facilitate discovery. However, detailed 
operational manuals or proprietary schematics remain protected by the innovator; access to 
these files is granted only to clients adopting the innovative technology. 
Clause 2.3: Non-Transfer of Rights. The act of uploading a technology to the Digital 
Platform does not constitute a transfer of ownership to the Platform or the System 
Administrator. Formal Technology Transfer Agreements (TTA) and licensing must be 
executed externally between the innovator and the client. 
3. System Security and Access Control 
Clause 3.1: Credential Responsibility. The User is solely responsible for maintaining the 
confidentiality of their login credentials. Any activity performed under a User’s account is 
deemed the action of that User. Users are required to notify the Administrator immediately 
upon any suspicion of unauthorized account access. 
Clause 3.2: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). To protect the integrity of the innovation 
marketplace, the Digital Platform requires MFA for all, ”Client”, "Innovator" and "Admin" 
roles. Users agree to provide a valid mobile number or authenticator app link to satisfy this 
security requirement. 
4. Administrative Moderation (E-Governance Compliance)

Clause 4.1: Verification of Innovative Technologies. In line with RA 12254 (E-
Governance Act), the System Administrator reserves the right to verify the legitimacy of any 
technology submitted for commercialization. Submissions that are found to be fraudulent, 
misleading, or scientifically unverified may be removed without prior notice. 
Clause 4.2: Role-Based Redirection. The system utilizes automated role-based redirection. 
Users agree to be categorized as "Client", "Innovator," or "Admin" based on the credentials 
and documentation provided during onboarding, and acknowledge that certain platform

Clause 5.1: "As-Is" Information. All technology listings are provided on an "as-is" basis. 
The Digital Platform does not verify the mechanical soundness or financial ROI of the 
technologies and shall not be held liable for any production losses or business failures 
resulting from an adoption facilitated through the marketplace. 
Clause 5.2: Third-Party Links. The Digital Platform may contain links to external DOST 
portals or official government filing systems. The Digital Platform is not responsible for the 
privacy practices or content of these external government sites.

Q6.  Why were these specific categories chosen for the platform? Please provide a brief 
description or rationale for each category — what it represents, what kinds of innovations fall 
under it, and what information or visuals should be highlighted on each category card on the 
landing page. 
This will be used to write the descriptive content displayed on each category card and section of the platform 
visible to guests, registered users, and innovators. 
Answer / Feedback: 
1. Agri-Aqua and Forestry 
Rationale: Northern Mindanao is the top producer of pineapple and a major producer of bananas and 
corn. This category captures the "raw material" side of the value chain.  
 
Innovations: Post-harvest facilities, smart farming sensors, high-yield seed varieties, and aquaculture 
feeding systems. 
 
Visuals/Info: Highlight "Yield Increase Percentage" or "Waste Reduction." Use icons or images of 
modern farm machinery or automated fisheries. 
 
2. Food Processing and Nutrition 
Rationale: This is the most critical category for your MSME study. Most local businesses in CDO and 
Bukidnon are in food manufacturing but struggle with shelf-life and packaging.

Innovations: Dehydrators, vacuum frying technology, retort processing (canning), and nutrient -fortified 
food formulations. 
 
Visuals/Info: Highlight "Shelf-life Extension" and "FDA Compliance Ready." Use visuals of stainless

3. Health and Medical Sciences 
Rationale: Aligns with DOST’s push for "Tuklas Lunas" (drug discovery from local plants) and

4. Energy, Utilities, and Environment 
Rationale: Region X has a heavy industrial base that requires sustainable energy and waste 
management solutions to lower operational costs. 
 
Innovations: Solar-powered irrigation, biomass energy from agricultural waste, and industrial 
wastewater treatment systems. 
 
Visuals/Info: Highlight "Energy Savings (kWh)" or "Carbon Footprint Reduction." Use icons of solar 
panels or green recycling arrows. 
 
5. Information and Communications Technology (ICT) 
Rationale: Supports the "Smart City" initiatives of Cagayan de Oro and the digitalization of logistics 
and supply chains in the region. 
 
Innovations: Inventory management systems, E-commerce integrations for MSMEs, and AI-driven 
predictive maintenance for factories. 
 
Visuals/Info: Highlight "Automation Level" and "System Interoperability." Use visuals of circuit patterns 
or cloud computing symbols. 
 
6. Advanced Manufacturing and Engineering 
Rationale: Reflects the "Metals and Engineering" strength of Iligan City and the surrounding areas. 
This is where heavy equipment and "Metal-works" innovations sit. 
 
Innovations: CNC machines, specialized metal casting, 3D industrial printing, and hydraulic press 
systems. 
 
Visuals/Info: Highlight "Production Speed (Units/Hour)" and "Material Durability." Use images of gears, 
mechanical arms, or metal fabrication.

SECTION 2 — GOOGLE DRIVE ASSET UPLOAD GUIDE 
The following instructions describe the folder structure and content requirements for all visual assets to be 
uploaded to the shared Google Drive. These assets will be used across the platform, including the landing page, 
category pages, marketplace, and administrative sections. 
 
NOTE:  All uploaded assets must be organized exactly as described below. Misplaced or improperly named 
files may result in them not being displayed correctly on the platform. Please do not alter the folder names. 
 
Google Drive Folder Structure 
 
Main Folder: Platform Assets 
• This is the root folder shared with the development team. 
• Do not modify the folder name or move files outside their designated subfolders. 
• Ensure that sharing permissions are set to "Anyone with the link can view" or provide direct access to 
the development team's Gmail account. 
 
Subfolder 1: /stock-photos 
Contains general-purpose photographs used on the landing page, hero banners, section backgrounds, and 
promotional materials. 
 
Subfolders within /stock-photos (one per category): 
• /stock-photos/Agriculture — Photos of crops, farmland, irrigation systems, farming technology, and 
rural innovation. 
• /stock-photos/Healthcare — Photos of medical facilities, health workers, medical devices, and 
community health programs. 
• /stock-photos/Energy — Photos of solar panels, wind turbines, sustainable energy infrastructure, and 
energy projects. 
• /stock-photos/Construction — Photos of structures, building materials, engineering projects, and 
construction innovation. 
• /stock-photos/Product Design — Photos of prototypes, product mockups, design labs, and 
manufactured goods. 
• /stock-photos/Information Technology — Photos of computers, software interfaces, networking 
equipment, and digital innovation. 
 
 
Subfolder 2: /products 
Contains media assets specifically associated with featured innovation posts on the marketplace. These images 
will be displayed directly on product listing cards and detail pages. 
 
Instructions for /products uploads: 
• Create one named subfolder per featured product or innovation (e.g., /products/SmartRiceMonitor). 
• Each product folder should contain at least 5 high-quality photographs of the product. 
• The first image in each folder will be treated as the cover thumbnail.

SECTION 3 — PRIVACY NOTICE & DATA HANDLING POLICY 
 
Data Privacy Notice 
In compliance with Republic Act No. 10173, otherwise known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012 , the platform is 
committed to protecting the privacy and security of all personal data collected through this platform.  
 
What Information Is Collected 
The platform collects the following categories of personal information during registration and account verification:  
• Gmail address 
• Mobile phone number 
• Government-issued identification document (image) 
• Platform activity data, including posts. 
 
How Your Information Is Used 
The personal data collected by the platform is used exclusively for the following purposes:  
• Verification of user identity and eligibility to participate on the platform  
• Administration of user accounts and platform access 
• Communication of account status updates, verification codes, and system notifications  
• Generation of platform analytics. 
 
Data Sharing & Disclosure 
Your personal data — including your identification documents and photographs — will NOT be shared 
with, sold to, or disclosed to any third party without your explicit written consent, except as required by 
applicable law or by order of a competent authority. 
 
By submitting this form and uploading assets to the shared Google Drive, you acknowledge that you have read and 
understood this Privacy Notice and that all uploaded materials are provided voluntarily for the development and operation 
of the platform.
